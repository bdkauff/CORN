From the toothpaste you use in the morning to the book you read at night, corn plays a part in nearly every aspect of our lives.
-corn.org


Corn comes in many forms: in drywall, in diapers, and in 3d printers. Toothpaste corn, for instance, is Sorbitol, 'synthesized by sorbitol-6-phosphate dehydrogenase, and converted to fructose by succinate dehydrogenase and sorbitol dehydrogenase.'

The majority of our submissions include corn in its most recognizable, iconic on-the-cob incarnation. This corn-- sweet, yellow, and uniform-- has a lovable immediacy. It's the corn most of us have internalized, literally and figuratively. A personal corn.

This corn--known corn, the hot-buttered, on-the-grill kind--accounts for its smallest use. Most corn goes unknown and unnoticed.

Corn asks: when did growth become overgrowth? How dangerous is the convenience of sameness?

As corn unknown spreads in a wild control (maybe insidiously, maybe not)getting bigger, taller, maybe ending with a real big pop, a certain corn remains immediate to us, still personal.
